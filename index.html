Bio

I am a final-year PhD student supervised by Josef Sivic in the Willow project-team of Inria Paris and in the Department of Computer Science of Ecole Normale Supérieure.
My main motivation is to develop algorithms that enable robots to perform complex manipulation tasks autonomously in unstructured environments using computer vision. My latest works have focused on recovering functional representations of scenes containing objects and robots. Other areas of interests include task-and-motion planning, synthetic data for training deep networks and robotics control.
Before doing my PhD, I graduated from Ecole Normale Supérieure de Cachan where I received a dual Masters degree in Mathematics, Machine Learning, Computer Vision (MVA) and in Electrical Engineering (E3A) with highest honors. In 2017, I was among the top candidates at the nationwide competition Agrégation in Electrical Engineering. I was an intern at Nvidia in 2022 and Chronocam (now Prophesee) in 2016.

News

I will graduate in January 2023. I am currently open to any opportunities for continuing to do exciting and ambitious research at the intersection of computer vision and robotics.
